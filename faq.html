Everything you need to know about our tallow products

What is tallow?

Beef tallow is rendered suet (leaf) fat from cattle. This most nutrient rich fat has been used for centuries as an excellent moisturiser because it's soft by nature, has a high lipid content and is uniquely bio compatible with our own skin cell structure allowing for seamless absorption compared to plant-based oils.

100% Grass-fed, chemical free tallow, is particularly beneficial, as it boasts a higher concentration of omega-3 fatty acids and antioxidants, to nourish & supporting skin health.

Cattle, who are frequently rotated through fresh & diverse, chemical free, nutrient rich pastures like ours are, enjoy improved health & welfare, and contribute to regenerative and sustainable farming practices that reduce environmental impact, and support bio diverse eco systems to thrive.

These practises result in a cleaner, safer product, free from hormones, antibiotics & chemicals—richer in beneficial nutrients.

We believe Organic Grass-fed Tallow is Natures skin food. It's safe for the whole family and an ideal choice for holistic & responsible skin care.

What is Our Tallow Made From?

The best tallow is made from the rich, 'Leaf fat' or 'Suet', which means the fat that directly surrounds the liver and kidney organs. It's the best fat to use because its richly dense in nutrients and minerals and is, 'soft' by nature. The best quality Leaf fat is derived from not only 100% grass-fed cattle, but also from organically raised cattle. This ensures the end product is of high quality & pure.

How is your tallow produced?

How Our Tallow is Made – With Love, By Hand

Fresh from the Source – We start with the freshest leaf fat, delivered the same day it's harvested.

Render with Care – The fat is chopped, grated, or minced, then slowly rendered at low temperatures using pure spring water that we hand-collect from a nearby natural spring.

4x Rendered – This careful rendering process is repeated four times until the fat becomes fully liquefied.

Filtered to Perfection – The liquid tallow is poured through a double-layered cheesecloth to remove impurities.

Settling Stage – Once set, the oil and water separate. We drain off the water and gently scrape away any remaining sediment.

Do It All Again – Yes, we repeat the process four times to achieve an ultra-clean, creamy, odourless tallow.

Blended with Goodness – The final tallow is reheated gently, then blended with nourishing oils like olive or almond, and infused with essential oils.

Let It Rest – We allow the blend to reset… tic toc tic toc.

Whipped to Perfection – Finally, we whip it into a silky, luxurious cream that looks good enough to eat — especially the vanilla-scented one!

This part never gets old. Watching it come together, filling the jars with soft, fragrant goodness, and hearing your stories of how much you love it — that's why we keep making it.

We hope you enjoy it just as much as we enjoy creating it for you.

Why do we only create Whipped Tallow Balms?

Because we believe Whipped Tallow is much better to use.

Its definitely lighter to apply, feels softer, creamier, is more luxurious and divine.

Whipped tallow is more versatile for a day and night cream, while many people attest to applying unwhipped balms only in the evening as they are heavier and sometimes greasier than a whipped version.

Sometimes we definitely wish we weren't so attached to the whipped balms though because they are much trickier to produce. They require 20% more processing time, can be less stable, more affected by temp changes, and re settle, a little lower in the jars at times.

They need to be made in smaller batches at a time to order rather than pre made in advance.

We have 95% sorted all these anomalies with extra care, work and processes, of course. Glass jars are massively helpful providing that extra insulation layer.

If you have any issues do let us know, our hope is that if you love the whip as much as us, a little re settling from time to time is a small price to pay!

Why do most Tallow companies only offer unwhipped?

Unwhipped tallow balms are easier to produce, more stable, less production cost, ie work/time. Requiring 20% less production time.

They are poured in jars/tins at a melting temp/warm to hot. (don't buy in aluminum)

They're easier to jar up, have a stable consistency and no further settling issues in the jar.

Whipping Tallow balms to us, is like heirloom fruits and veg you can only enjoy if you grow your own, because they aren't as commercially viable like produce that can be pre-picked, pre-made in large batches, and stored for a longer period of time.
